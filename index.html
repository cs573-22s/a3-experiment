Introduction of the experiment

The original thought of this experiment came from William Cleveland and Robert McGill's paper in 1984 published the results of several controlled experiments that pitted bar charts against pies and stacked-bar variants.

In this experiment, You will be given a different type of data visualizations per question. For each question, you are asked to estimate the proportion of a smaller shape to the larger one. You are not supposed to use measurement tools, but just make a quickly estimate and fill in your guess for the proportion and move on to the next question. For a better experience, please use a laptop or computer instead of of a mobile device. The test will take approximately 10-15 minutes. We guarantee that all data will be used only for result analysis and will not be used anywhere outside the course.
